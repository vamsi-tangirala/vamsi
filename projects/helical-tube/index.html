Performance Analysis of a Hexagonal Twisted Capillary Tube in Refrigerators

April 3, 2026 · 

Overview#

This project investigates the thermal performance of a hexagonal twisted capillary tube aimed at improving the efficiency of domestic refrigeration systems.

A comparative computational study was conducted across four geometries:

  • Straight tube
  • Elliptical tube
  • Plus-shaped twisted tube
  • Hexagonal twisted tube

The goal was to identify the optimal geometry for enhanced heat dissipation and system performance.

Objective#

  • Maximize heat transfer efficiency through geometry optimization
  • Enhance flow characteristics for improved thermal exchange
  • Reduce compressor power consumption
  • Increase system lifespan by minimizing mechanical strain

Background#

Capillary tubes act as the throttling device in vapor compression refrigeration systems, connecting high-pressure and low-pressure regions.

Conventional designs (straight or helical) often require higher refrigerant usage, leading to:

  • increased energy consumption
  • environmental impact due to refrigerants

This study explores how complex internal geometries and induced turbulence can:

  • enhance heat transfer
  • reduce refrigerant demand
  • improve system efficiency

Methodology#

  • Designed four capillary tube geometries with identical length (100 mm)
  • Applied consistent boundary conditions:
    • Fluid temperature: 30°C
    • Ambient temperature: 25°C
  • Used a heat transfer coefficient of 5000 W/(m²·K) to simulate turbulent flow conditions
  • Conducted thermal simulations to evaluate:
    • heat flux
    • temperature distribution
    • thermal gradients

Results#

Hexagonal Twisted Tube (Best Performance)#

  • Highest heat transfer efficiency
  • Increased surface area and internal complexity
  • Improved thermal mixing

Thermal Behavior#

  • Stronger thermal gradients → faster cooling response
  • Reduced thermal resistance
  • Higher heat flux handling capability

Comparison#

  • Straight tube → lowest performance
  • Twisted geometries → significantly improved heat transfer
  • Hexagonal design → optimal balance of performance and flow dynamics

Key Learnings#

  • Geometry plays a critical role in thermal system performance
  • Surface area enhancement directly improves heat transfer
  • Induced turbulence significantly boosts thermal efficiency
  • Trade-off exists between performance and manufacturability

Tools Used#

  • Autodesk Fusion 360 (thermal and flow simulation)
  • CAD modeling for geometric design
  • Engineering analysis using conservation equations
