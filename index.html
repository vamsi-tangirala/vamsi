Vamsi Krishna Tangirala#

Mechanical engineering student working in the areas of thermal engineering, computational fluid dynamics (CFD), and thermal-fluid systems.

My work focuses on understanding and optimizing heat transfer and fluid flow behavior in engineered systems through numerical simulation, theoretical analysis, and computational modeling.

Research Interests#

My interests lie in transport phenomena in thermal-fluid systems, particularly in the interaction between fluid mechanics and heat transfer.

Areas I am currently exploring include:

  • Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)
  • Heat transfer enhancement mechanisms
  • Flow–structure interactions in heat exchangers
  • Refrigeration system optimization
  • Energy-efficient cooling technologies

Current Work#

I am currently investigating the effect of externally introduced transverse vibrations on heat exchanger performance.

The objective is to analyze how mechanically induced oscillations influence flow structures, turbulence characteristics, and heat transfer behavior within thermal systems.

Simulations are performed using OpenFOAM-based CFD frameworks, focusing on:

  • velocity field evolution
  • turbulence characteristics
  • temperature distribution
  • variation of heat transfer coefficients
